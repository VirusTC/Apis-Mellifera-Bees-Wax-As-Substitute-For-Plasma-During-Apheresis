Standard Operating Procedure (SOP): Safe Thermal Hydrolysis & Organic Emulsification

**Document ID:** SOP-ORG-EMUL-001\
**Application:** Topical Cosmetic and Skin-Barrier Formulations\
**Process Type:** Thermal Hydrolysis, Mechanical Homogenization, and Organic Preservation

* * * * *

1\. Process Overview & Objectives

The primary objective of this procedure is to break down the dense, hydrophobic structure of raw beeswax and integrate it cleanly with a blue corn lipid phase and a 3% honey-water matrix.

Rather than using synthetic chemicals or strong acids, this protocol relies on **water-and-heat hydrolysis** to loosen wax ester chains, followed by high-shear mechanical action to force the lipid and aqueous phases into a permanent, smooth, and shelf-stable topical cream.

 [Phase A: Heated Lipid Mix]\
                               --> [High-Shear Blending (65°C)] --> [Controlled Cooling] --> [Stable Topical Base]

2\. Raw Material Specifications & Formulation Ratios

To ensure the mixture achieves an ideal cosmetic texture without separating on the shelf, the ingredients are divided into a **Lipid Phase (Phase A)** and an **Aqueous Phase (Phase B)**.

| Phase | Component | Target Percentage (by Weight) | Function |
| **Phase A** | Purified Beeswax (*Apis mellifera*) | 15.0% | Hydrophobic structural barrier, thickener |
| **Phase A** | Blue Corn Lipid Extract (Triglycerides) | 25.0% | Emollient, nutrient-rich lipid carrier |
| **Phase A** | Sunflower or Soy Lecithin (Organic) | 2.0% | Natural amphiphilic emulsifier |
| **Phase B** | Distilled Water (Deionized) | 55.0% | Solvent base, hydration medium |
| **Phase B** | Raw African Honey (*A. m. scutellata*) | 3.0% | Natural co-solvent, humectant, stabilizer |

* * * * *

3\. Step-by-Step Processing Instructions

Step 1: Aqueous Phase Hydration and Pre-Heating (Phase B)

1.  Measure the distilled water into a clean, stainless steel jacketed heating vessel.
2.  Heat the water to exactly **66.67°C**.
3.  Slowly add the 3% raw African honey into the warm water. Stir continuously at low speed (100--200 RPM) until the honey sugars are completely dissolved.
4.  *Scientific Note:* Keeping the honey solution at this controlled temperature prevents the destruction of delicate, heat-sensitive enzymes (like glucose oxidase) while allowing the fructose and glucose to form stable hydrogen bonds with the water molecules.

Step 2: Thermal Hydrolysis of the Lipid Phase (Phase A)

1.  In a separate heated vessel, combine the solid beeswax chunks, blue corn lipids, and organic lecithin.
2.  Gradually raise the temperature of the lipid blend to **80.00°C** to fully melt the wax.
3.  Once molten, hold the mixture at **80.00°C for 20 to 30 minutes** under gentle agitation.
4.  *Scientific Note:* This extended thermal treatment in the presence of the lipid moisture induces a mild **thermal hydrolysis**. This breaks down a fraction of the heavy, long-chain wax esters into shorter-chain free fatty acids and fatty alcohols, significantly increasing their ability to interface with water.

Step 3: High-Shear Emulsification & Blending

1.  Slowly pour the molten **Phase A (80°C)** directly into the vortex of the heated **Phase B (66.67°C)**.
2.  Immediately engage an industrial colloid mill or a high-shear overhead homogenizer.
3.  Process the combined mixture at **3,000 to 4,500 RPM for 10 to 15 minutes**, maintaining an internal batch temperature of roughly **70°C**.
4.  *Scientific Note:* The mechanical shear forces the molten beeswax and corn oil to fracture into microscopic droplets (under 5 microns). The dissolved honey sugars and lecithin molecules instantly wrap around these droplets, creating a highly stable colloidal suspension.

Step 4: Controlled Cooling and Structural Setting

1.  Reduce the homogenizer speed to a gentle sweep-mix setting (50--100 RPM).
2.  Allow the vessel to cool gradually down to ** room temperature (20°C--22°C)**. Do not flash-freeze the mixture, as rapid freezing forces ice crystals to separate from the fat matrix.
3.  As the mixture cools past the beeswax solidification point (~62°C), it will transform from a fluid into a smooth, pearlescent, non-greasy cosmetic barrier lotion.

* * * * *

4\. Quality Control, Stability & Storage Protocols

-   **Microbial Control (Fermentation Prevention):** Because this organic fluid contains rich starches and sugars from the corn and honey, it must be protected from wild airborne yeasts. Package the final cream immediately into airtight, sterile cosmetic pumps or tubes.
-   **Shelf-Life Optimization:** To keep the product fresh and mold-free without using toxic heavy metals like copper, you can add 1% **Leucidal Liquid** (a clean peptide preservative derived from fermented radishes) during the final cooling phase (below 40°C).
-   **Storage Parameters:** Store the finished topical product in a cool, dry place between **15°C and 25°C**. Avoid direct sunlight, which can oxidize the natural pigments in the blue corn lipids and fade the color of the cream over time.
