### 2. Phase-by-Phase Engineering Requirements

### Phase A: The Plasma Scaffold (Apis Mellifera Repository)
*   **Problem:** Raw beeswax is a hydrophobic solid. Placed directly in the bloodstream, it causes lethal vascular occlusion.
*   **Solution:** Controlled **Saponification** via alkaline titration (NaOH/KOH) to convert free fatty acids into water-soluble amphiphilic micellar structures. This must be buffered with recombinant human albumin derived from *Oryza sativa* to maintain colloidal osmotic pressure (25 mmHg).

### Phase B: The Micro-Lipid Carrier (Plant-Based Lipid Capsules Repository)
*   **Problem:** Lipids naturally coalesce in water, creating large droplets that trigger pulmonary fat embolisms.
*   **Solution:** High-shear homogenization using **Soy Lecithin** as a surfactant surfactant. Lipids must be encapsulated in a stable monolayer with a strictly controlled particle diameter between **100nm and 200nm** to freely pass through human capillaries.

### Phase C: Oxygen Conveyance (VerduraRX Beta-Carotene Repository)
*   **Problem:** Beta-carotene possesses no native atomic iron or globin structure to reversibly bind molecular oxygen ($O_2$). It is highly prone to photo-oxidation.
*   **Solution:** The carotene must be embedded within an aqueous protein carrier matrix—specifically **Soy Leghemoglobin**—utilizing a hydrophobic pocket to safely facilitate gas exchange without releasing toxic peroxides into the bloodstream.

## 3. Critical Safety Tolerances
*   **Viscosity:** 3.5 to 5.5 cP (centipoise) at 37°C to match native human blood.
*   **Osmolality:** 285–295 mOsm/kg H2O.
*   **Zeta Potential:** Must maintain a charge <-30mV to prevent spontaneous droplet aggregation.
